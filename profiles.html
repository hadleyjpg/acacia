introducing abby hamilton

by Rachel White

Do you ever wonder what life is like for that one friend of yours from college? You know, the one who had the guts to chase their dreams, who committed to making music or writing novels for a living, who hung their diploma on the wall and never looked back. For me, that friend is Abby Hamilton: a country music singer-songwriter based in Lexington, Kentucky. One year out from her college graduation, she will be the first person to tell you the truth about what it’s like to kick off a career in music as a young, unestablished artist.

adjusting to the space between cultures

by Cathryn Lien

Art has the power to communicate beyond cultural and language barriers. Lydia Underwood, a senior art major from Lexington, Kentucky, plans to teach English as a Second Language in China after graduating from college. Yet for her, connecting with people on a global scale begins not with English but with watercolors, printmaking and typography.

amena brown: poet, author, teller of truths

by Anna Leigh Morrow

When I first heard Amena Brown speak at a university-wide convocation during my senior year of college, I was immediately struck by her gift for storytelling. Her speaking style is engaging, honest, humorous and moving. She has a knack not just for spoken word poetry, which is how she built a name for herself, but also for telling prose accounts of her rich personal and family history. After convocation ended, I attended an additional poetry performance by Amena so that I could hear more of her work. I wasn’t disappointed. She has a skill with words and a gift for sharing them, for speaking directly into the hearts of her audience.

rosanna willhite: bold, beautiful, badass

by Sarah Browning

Meet Rosanna Willhite. She works as Coordinator of the Global Engagement Office at Asbury University in Wilmore, Kentucky. She has Bachelor’s degrees in English Literature and Spanish Language from Berea College as well as an M.A. in English as a Second Language from the University of Kentucky. Her ferocity, confidence, flare and poise are immediately striking to everyone who meets her.
